## Too hot to work. Heat strike anyone?

How are you coping with the heat? As I write this, indoor temperatures are approaching 28˚C, which is apparently the maximum to safely perform ‘light indoors office work’, [according to the Arboportaal](https://www.arboportaal.nl/vraag-en-antwoord/alle-vragen-en-antwoorden/wat-is-te-warm). If your job involves laying fibre-optic cables or other forms of very strenuous physical activity, then you should only work at temperatures of at most 23˚C (or 25˚C if there’s a significant breeze). (Note, these are *recommended* levels, not legally binding maximums.)

![Heatwave forecast for the Netherlands](knmi.png)
<figcaption style="text-align: center;"><i>Meteorological agency KNMI's forecast for dangerous heat levels in late June 2026</i></figcaption>

Working in a too-hot workplace can be dangerous. Exhaustion can leave you and your coworkers unable to concentrate, which could lead to errors and injury. [You could risk heat-related conditions](https://www.arboportaal.nl/onderwerpen/fysische-factoren/warmte) such as dehydration, heat stroke, or even death. Heat is becoming a daily occupational hazard for many workers in the Netherlands, and realistically, it’s only going to get worse.

If it’s too hot at your workplace, your boss [may have to take measures](https://www.arboportaal.nl/onderwerpen/fysische-factoren/warmte/wat-zegt-de-wet), such as: offering you personal protective equipment, chilled drinks, shortening hours or even scrapping work entirely for that day. You’re allowed to consult with the _arbodienst_ (occupational health and safety agency) about measures.

But what if your boss doesn’t act? In the UK, a coalition of labour unions and environmental action groups including the Trades Union Congress, the Bakers, Food and Allied Workers Union, the Fire Brigades Union, and Extinction Rebellion are calling for legally binding maximum temperatures, and call on workers to [enact a heat strike](https://www.thecanary.co/uk/news/2026/06/24/heat-strike-coalition/) should temperature rise above certain levels. Idea?

## On the radar

Some news crumbs that caught tech workers’ attention this month:

- Strikes! Workers at dairy merchant [FrieslandCampina Leeuwarden](https://effat.org/in-the-spotlight/friesland-campina-leeuwarden-workers-strike-for-fair-compensation/) are striking for a 4% wage increase, workers at liquor store [Gall & Gall](https://www.asatunews.co.id/en/gall-gall-employees-strike-sunday-pay) strike against a ca. 5% pay cut, and [public transport](https://nltimes.nl/2026/06/19/ns-trains-4-hours-wednesday-workers-strike-social-benefits-cuts) workers against proposed cuts to social security.
- Offices of the student labour union [ASVA in Amsterdam were vandalised](https://techwerkers.nl/en/posts/asva-aanval-studentenvakbond/) in the early hours of Saturday 21 June. ASVA chair Sahand Mozdbar suspects the attack was targeted, but said the workers won’t let themselves be intimidated.
- Labour union history: High school student Christian Martina interviews worker Wim van Seeters about their role in the [1969 unionized working youth protest in Den Haag](https://vakbondshistorie.nl/dossiers/wim-van-seeters-over-de-kwj/), for the right to education and better wages.
- If LLMs have human-like attributes, then [so does Age of Empires II](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.31514).
- Need to identify yourself to the Dutch government? First create a Google account or Apple ID, please. At least … that’s what the [Dutch government thinks is fine for NL Wallet](https://archive.is/aR8YC), the alleged successor to the current DigiD system.
- [Norway bans the use of LLMs](https://www.security.nl/posting/941488/Noorwegen+verbiedt+AI+in+basisonderwijs%3A+%27Kinderen+moeten+eigen+antwoorden+vinden%27) in primary education, because 'children should find their own answers'.
- Who’s delaying pay transparency? [The Netherlands is delaying pay transparency!](https://www.etuc.org/en/pressrelease/just-three-countries-meet-pay-transparency-deadline) (until at least 2027)
- [Dutch Ministry of Defense is setting up partnerships with higher education institutions](https://%20%20https://konfrontatie.nl/thema-s/oorlog-en-ontwapening/militarisering-van-het-onderwijs) to stimulate student recruitment into the military, yet the Algemene Onderwijsbond fails to take a stance.
- Spending billions on weapons, yet supposedly the Netherlands [has 'no cash' to maintain the current air raid alert system](https://www.security.nl/posting/940003/Kabinet+gaat+luchtalarm+vervangen+door+'nieuw+innovatief+sirenenetwerk')?
- The Bijstandsbond union for people on low incomes [turns 50 this year](https://solidariteit.nl/overgenomen/2026/o566-2_sociale-solidariteit-is-een-keuze.html). Congratulations!
