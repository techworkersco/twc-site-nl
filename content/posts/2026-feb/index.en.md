## Spotlight: Layoffs at ASML

On 28 January managers at semiconductor producer ASML, based in Veldhoven (Noord-Brabant, NL), [announced that](https://www.asml.com/en/news/press-releases/2026/strengthening-focus-on-engineering-and-innovation) they're going fire around 1,700 workers. ASML managers chose mass layoffs, even though they admit that business is going well. Really well. What's going on here? 

Companies use layoffs as a short-term cost-cutting tool, often to placate shareholders. Instead of distributing increased profits back to the workers who actually created that wealth, workers are given the sack. 

ASML is just the latest big company in the Netherlands to pull this trick. [Meta](https://www.unie.nl/en/jouw-cao-en-branche/reorganisatie-bij-meta) and [Booking](https://www.nu.nl/economie/6362691/bookingcom-gaat-zon-duizend-werknemers-ontslaan-honderden-in-nederland.html) did the same last year.

Are you fed up with doing all the work, and then being treated as disposable? Get organized with other tech workers!
